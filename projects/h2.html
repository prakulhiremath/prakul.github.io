Green H₂

Overview

Green H₂ is a thermally driven hydrogen production system based on concentrated solar power (CSP). The system prioritizes high-temperature solar thermal input instead of photovoltaic-driven electrolysis.

Research Domain

Energy Systems → Solar Thermal → Hydrogen Production

Core Principle

Concentrated solar radiation is used to generate high-temperature heat. This thermal energy drives hydrogen production pathways, reducing dependence on electrical conversion losses associated with PV systems.

System Architecture

• Solar concentration field (CSP mirrors or heliostat system)
• High-temperature thermal receiver
• Thermal-driven hydrogen generation chamber
• Corrosion-resistant catalysts
• High-stability membranes for gas separation
• Hydrogen storage interface

Materials Focus

The system investigates corrosion-resistant catalysts and thermally stable membrane materials capable of operating under high-temperature and chemically reactive environments.

Status

Conceptual system modeling and materials evaluation in progress.

Future Work

• Thermal efficiency modeling
• Catalyst durability testing
• Membrane longevity under cyclic stress
• Integration with storage and downstream applications
